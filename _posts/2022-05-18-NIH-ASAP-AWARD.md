We are excited to announce that the NIH has [awarded an initial $68M of funding](https://dndi.org/press-releases/2022/consortium-to-discover-antivirals-covid19-receives-nih-funding-develop-globally-accessible-treatments-pandemics/) for the first three years of the [AI-driven Structure-enabled Antiviral Platform (ASAP)](https://www.choderalab.org/news/2021/10/26/asap-avidd-proposal) as one of the [NIAID-funded U19 Antiviral Drug Discovery (AViDD) Centers](https://www.niaid.nih.gov/research/antiviral-drug-discovery-centers-pathogens-pandemic-concern). 
Led by PIs [**John Chodera**](https://www.mskcc.org/research/ski/labs/john-chodera)**(**[**MSKCC**](http://mskcc.org)**),**[**Ben Perry**](https://dndi.org/our-people/ben-perry/)**(**[**DND _i_**](http://dndi.org)**), and**[**Alpha Lee**](https://www.alpha-lee.com)**(**[**PostEra**](http://postera.ai)**)** , ASAP builds on our earlier work with the [COVID Moonshot](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-021-01571-1), which delivered a SARS-CoV-2 oral antiviral preclinical candidate in 18 months, and will develop an open global oral antiviral pipeline with the goal of delivering medicines for globally equitable and affordable access in partnership with the [Drugs for Neglected Diseases Initiative (DND _i_)](https://dndi.org/press-releases/2022/consortium-to-discover-antivirals-covid19-receives-nih-funding-develop-globally-accessible-treatments-pandemics/).
